Partnerships

You own outreach, relationship-building, and brand collaboration for Ruelle. Jarvis is your research and drafting engine — you bring the relationships, Jarvis handles the prep work.

Introduce yourself

What You Own

Your Scope

  • Brand collaborations and influencer outreach
  • Church and ministry partnerships for Lightning + Ruelle
  • Wholesale and retail partner conversations
  • Investor and accelerator pipeline (Praxis, OCEAN, Techstars)
  • Tracking and following up on open conversations
What to Use Jarvis For
📨

Outreach Drafts

Give Jarvis a name, their platform/brand, and what you want. Jarvis writes the first draft. You refine and send.

🔍

Research

Ask Jarvis to research a brand, creator, or organization before you reach out. Get background, audience size, alignment with Ruelle's mission.

📋

Proposals & Decks

Jarvis can draft partnership proposals, one-pagers, and talking points. Bring the deal idea — Jarvis builds the document.

📊

Pipeline Tracking

Tell Jarvis where each conversation stands. Jarvis keeps notes and reminds you when to follow up.

Example Prompts
"Draft a cold outreach email to [creator name] about a Ruelle collab. They focus on Christian lifestyle content, ~50k followers on IG."
"Research Praxis Accelerator — what do they look for, what's the application timeline, and what should Jordyn know before applying?"
"Write a partnership one-pager for Ruelle targeting faith-based boutique retailers."
"I had a call with [brand] today. Here's what we discussed: [notes]. What are the next steps and what should I follow up on?"
Requires Jordyn's Approval

Always get sign-off before:

  • Sending any external email or message on behalf of Ruelle
  • Making any commitment — financial, timeline, or deliverable
  • Sharing pricing, partnership terms, or brand assets
  • Reaching out to investors or accelerator programs
Brand Rules — Never Use These Words

Off-Limits Vocabulary

  • blessed — overused, feels performative
  • amazing — generic filler
  • iconic — not Ruelle's voice
  • fire / lit / slay — not aligned with brand tone
Weekly Rhythm

Cadence

  • Monday — Review open outreach threads, plan the week's contacts
  • Wednesday — Check in on any pending responses, send follow-ups
  • Friday — Update Jarvis on pipeline status so it carries into next week

What You Own

Your Scope

  • Brand voice consistency across all touchpoints

What to Use Jarvis For
✏️

Product Copy

Describe the product — fabric, feel, story behind it — and Jarvis writes the copy. Grounded in Ruelle's voice: authentic, bold, spiritually rooted.

Campaign Concepts

Tell Jarvis the drop, the season, the feeling you're going for. Get 3–5 concept directions to choose from.

"Give me 5 campaign concept directions for a spring drop called 'New Creation.' Target: 16–30 year old Christians."
"Write a product launch email for the Signature Collection jackets. Keep it short, bold, no hype language."
"Write a creative brief for a product shoot — 3 looks, natural light, urban setting, faith-forward aesthetic."
Requires Jordyn's Approval

Always get sign-off before:

  • Publishing or sending any copy externally
  • Introducing a new tagline or campaign name
  • Changing product names or descriptions in Shopify
  • Any copy that touches faith/scripture — tone must be reviewed
Brand Voice — The Rules

Ruelle Sounds Like

  • Authentic — real stories, real faith, no performance
  • Bold — says what it means, doesn't hedge
  • Spiritually grounded — faith is the foundation, never the aesthetic
  • Direct — short sentences, strong verbs, no filler

Never Use

  • blessed, amazing, iconic, fire, slay, lit
  • Corporate or polished marketing language
  • Anything that sounds like a brand trying too hard
Target Audience

Who You're Writing For

  • Age 16–30, Christian, not ashamed of their faith
  • They want clothing that means something — not just aesthetic
  • They've lived something. Their testimony is real.

Platform Notes

Instagram (@ruelleclub)

  • No markdown — plain text only in captions
  • Use line breaks for readability, not headers
  • Hashtags: 5–10 max, relevant, no spam tags
  • Stories can be more casual and behind-the-scenes
