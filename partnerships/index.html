Role Guide

Partnerships

You own outreach, relationship-building, and brand collaboration for Ruelle. Jarvis is your research and drafting engine — you bring the relationships, Jarvis handles the prep work.

First Week Checklist

Download Telegram — free on iPhone and Android

Open your agent link — Jordyn will send this to you directly

Send your intro message — "Hi, I'm [name], Ruelle Partnerships"

Read the Brand Rules section — know the words that are off-limits

Run your first test — ask Jarvis to draft an outreach email to a brand you have in mind

Review the approval process — know what needs Jordyn's sign-off before it leaves your hands

Getting Access — Step by Step
1

Download Telegram

Free on iPhone and Android. Just a phone number to register — no extra accounts required.

2

Open your agent link

Jordyn will send you the Partnerships agent link. Tap it, then hit Start.

3

Send your intro

First message: "Hi, I'm [your name], Ruelle Partnerships." Jarvis will confirm and you're live.

4

You're in

No further setup needed. Message Jarvis whenever you need something. Treat it like texting a very smart teammate.

Your Scope

What You Own

  • Brand collaborations and influencer outreach
  • Church and ministry partnerships for Lightning + Ruelle
  • Wholesale and retail partner conversations
  • Investor and accelerator pipeline (Praxis, OCEAN, Techstars)
  • Tracking open conversations and following up
Daily & Weekly Workflow

Monday

Wednesday

  • Check for pending responses

  • Research any new targets you're considering

Friday

  • Update Jarvis on where each conversation stands

What to Use Jarvis For
📨

Outreach Drafts

Give Jarvis a name, their platform/brand, and your goal. Jarvis writes the draft — you refine and send.

🔍

Research

Ask Jarvis to research a brand or creator before you reach out. Background, audience, and mission alignment.

📋

Proposals & One-Pagers

Describe the deal — Jarvis builds the document. Partnership proposals, pitch decks, talking points.

📊

Pipeline Notes

Tell Jarvis where each conversation stands. It keeps notes and reminds you when to follow up.

"Draft a cold outreach to [creator name]. They do Christian lifestyle content, ~50k IG followers. We want to explore a Ruelle collab."

"Write a follow-up to someone I emailed 2 weeks ago about a collab. No response yet. Short and warm."
The Approval Process
1

Jarvis drafts it

You ask, Jarvis produces the email, proposal, or message.

2

You review it

Read it over. Ask Jarvis to revise anything that doesn't feel right.

3

Send to Jordyn

Forward the final draft with context: who it's going to and why.

4

Wait for approval

Nothing goes out until Jordyn approves. Flag urgency clearly if needed.

5

Send and log it

Send the message, then tell Jarvis it went out so the pipeline stays current.

What Always Requires Jordyn's Approval

Never send without sign-off:

  • Any external email or message on behalf of Ruelle
  • Any commitment — financial, timeline, or deliverable
  • Sharing pricing, partnership terms, or brand assets
  • Reaching out to investors or accelerator programs
  • Anything that could bind Ruelle to an agreement
Brand Rules

Never Use These Words

  • blessed — overused, performative
  • amazing — generic filler
  • iconic — not Ruelle's voice
  • fire / lit / slay — not on brand
Ruelle sounds like: authentic, bold, spiritually grounded. Real stories. No performance. Direct language. Faith is the foundation — not the aesthetic.
